About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD student in the Department of Mathematics at Brown University. My advisor is Bena Tshishiku.

I am broadly interested in low-dimensional topology and geometric group theory. Recently I have been interested in equivariant problems in the theory of mapping class groups. I have also been thinking about translation surfaces and their SL(2,R)-orbits in moduli space.

Before coming to Brown, I was an undergraduate at the University of Virginia where I studied math and computer science. My undergraduate thesis advisor was Sara Maloni.

Research

Papers and preprints

  1. Veech fibrations
    with Sam Freedman, in preparation.

    We investigate complex surfaces that fiber over Teichmüller curves with generic fiber a Veech surface. In most cases, they are minimal surfaces of general type. We compute the topological and complex-geometric invariants of these surfaces via the monodromy action on the mod-m homology of the fiber. We get exact values of the invariants for all known algebraically primitive Teichmüller curves.

  2. Homological representations of low genus mapping class groups (pdf, arxiv)
    Submitted.

    In this paper, we study homological representations of mapping class groups. Given a finite group G acting on a surface S, we consider the question of whether the representation of Mod(S)^G given by the action on H_1(S;Q) has arithmetic image. This question has been answered both positively and negatively in different cases by the work of several authors. Our main result is a complete answer to this question when S has genus at most 3.
